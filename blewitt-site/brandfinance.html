REPORTS

The reports are the company’s main selling point, as they convey inside all the services that Brand Finance, in the form of researches and adverts, making this publication an easy tool to hand out to potential clients. The reports are divided in three categories: nation reports, sector reports, and special reports. As Graphic Designer Manager, I led the whole chain of production of finance reports. I handled the entire production process from end to end to successfully communicate the brand’s identity. This involved scheduling material to receive from the different departments (finance analysts and copywriters), generating graphs and tables from Excel documents, assembling reports, sourcing appropriate stock imagery and clip art images – retouching and editing to make them print-ready where necessary – and liaising with the printer.

By redesigning the report’s template, I was accountable for streamlining the whole process, cutting down the production time from an average of two days to three hours. This is because I was constantly researching if there was a more efficient way to achieve a result. I ended up creating several macros for excel and for GREP, creating a few fonts for placing the icons, recreated from scratch the report in a modular structure, and opportunely connecting the text frames in InDesign. Plus, I must mention that AutoHotkey really improved my efficiency. DRY, no more WET.






SOFT•POWER

This has been a really big event, with an audience of over 400 international diplomats and with Ban Ki-moon, 8th Secretary-General of the United Nations as key speaker. This has been organised by Brand Finance to launch their Soft Power Index research, for which they have interviewed over 55,000 people from a hundred countries in order to rank the perceptions of sixty nations. It invited the global public and just over a thousand specialist experts to rank these sixty nations against specific criteria.
I have made the graphic project, all the graphs and tables inside, page it and liaised with the printer for a batch of 1000 copies.

In this picture, Ban Ki-Moon, 8th Secretary-General of the United Nations holding the report.

FOOTBALL•​ANNUAL

The Brand Finance Football Annual 2019 is a hard-back coffee table book about the business of football, available on Amazon. It features detailed club brand profiles and provides in-depth analysis on club brand values, enterprise values, sponsorship effectiveness, league reputation, and stadia performance. It includes special contributions by LaLiga, Juventus, and Barclays, among others. It’s an insightful book meant to be shared internally and with external stakeholders – either gifted or strategically placed at corporate hospitality events. I have made the graphic project, all the graphs and tables inside, page it and liaised with the printer for a batch of 1000 copies. As well I was personally in touch with the graphic teams of LaLiga, Juventus and BuroHappold.
